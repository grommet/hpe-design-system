## Using background and foreground colors

Backgrounds pair with text and icon colors, unless they have a specific color pairing. For example, `color.background.default` or `color.background.front` will work with `color.text.default` or `color.icon.strong`.

## Accessible color pairings

These background colors can be paired with the following text and icon colors and will meet accessibility requirements for color contrast.

Combine background and text and icon colors with confidence as all contrast ratios have been tested.

## Strong background and onStrong colors

Strong backgrounds have higher emphasis and are always paired with onStrong colors. This ensures that content sitting on strong backgrounds, for example, text and icons, will always be visible and accessible, even if colors are updated at a future date.

## Specialty color pairings

Some colors have pairings with added granular definition. For example, `color.background.primary.strong` is paired with `color.text.onPrimaryStrong`.

Only some colors are chosen for this specific level of pairing, in order to enable the styling management from the token level.
